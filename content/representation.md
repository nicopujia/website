This website[^1] hasn't always looked like this, nor developed with this technology, nor had this content. Such decisions, now that I'm reflecting, represent me pretty well.

### Simple

To begin with, it's made with a [Pelican](https://getpelican.com/), a minimalistic Python <abbr title="Static Site Generator">SSG</abbr>. Using libraries like React.js for this would be an awful bloat. Even if sending [7KB](https://bundlephobia.com/package/react@19.2.4) of JavaScript won't change anything to the end user, I have to [sleep well at night](https://www.goodreads.com/quotes/445621-when-you-re-a-carpenter-making-a-beautiful-chest-of-drawers). This is not a web app.

Apart from that, are you familiar with those flashy personal portfolio [websites](https://github.com/emmabostian/developer-portfolios?tab=readme-ov-file) from Indian guys, filled with blue-to-pink gradient backgrounds? This website also acts as a way of being against those complex designs, which brings me to the next point.

Making something simpler means distilling it to its essence. If you have a desk full of clutter and your Mac, and the only thing you use is your Mac, simplifying it would mean keeping just the Mac. 

So the point is, why care about simplicity? 

It comes down to the purpose of what you create. Having the non-simplest version of something means there's clutter around it. Clutter, in a website, is visual noise. Noise degrades a communication channel. What's this website for if not communicating? To express my ideas for myself—yeah, that too. But for that, I have my personal, messy notes; it is this website's purpose to be an effective communicational channel.

However, achieving simplicity is not that easy. Recognizing clutter is what designers do for a living. A good rule of thumb to know if you're improving is looking at your past work. If you don't think it's disgusting in some way, watch out.

As the Zen of Python says:

> Simple is better than complex.

Let's move on. 

### Effective

To reach simple writing you first need some writing. So when I'm dumping my ideas, I usually do it in lowercase because it's faster to transcribe my thoughts. It's said that lowercase signals familiarity, relaxed writing, etc. Why, then, isn't this article written in lowercase, which is how I write? 

If you've ever enganged with a novel, you've noticed that the familiarity aspect comes from the wording and from your relationship with the narrative, so lowercase isn't a requirement for that. You could argue that while it's not a requirement, it can improve the familiarity aspect anyway. And even if it did, the argument isn't strong enough, because writing with sentence case makes reading easier for the eyes; and easier reading means more effective communication. It's the same reason I chose a simple sans-serif font for content, and only use serif for headings.

### Aiming high

> 18 y/o founder 🇦🇷 | building [whatever]

I have admittedly done a similar thing in the past with my headline, and while it works in a way, I prefer avoiding it.

At first, it calls the reader's attention, as the average 18-year-old isn't a founder, so you are incentivized to do it from that side. By doing so, though, you're saying "look at how young I am." (Otherwise, the implied message wouldn't be about being young, so you would see most 34-year-old founders doing the same thing, and they're not.) 

But even if that happens, what do you want to call the attention for? Is it for your great skills and outstanding projects, or because you started working before the average? Or do you just want attention in general? If so, there are [more effective ways](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shock_advertising).

The reason I prefer avoiding it is because I want to aim higher than the age metric. 

Have you ever heard Vitalik bragging about his young age? He was too focused building the world's second-largest blockchain. It's even better for your public image if you let others discover and talk about your age, after they know about your great work, rather than you showing it upfront.

Besides, using it justifies whatever you haven't achieved yet, because you're showing your potential over your great work (which probably even doesn't exist yet, or you wouldn't use the age metric). In short, it's a [dangerous, easy, safe, and lazy](https://gadiborovich.substack.com/p/escaping-my-age) way to describe yourself. If you're going to brag, make it meaty.
 
### Latest = best
 
No matter how good I think it currently looks is, I currently don't know how, but I'll probably dislike some part of this website's design and of this article some time in the future. The latest version always seems to me like the best one, until it becomes ugly, and I feel the urge to update it. So it again seems like the best one.

Will I ever come to a final state that satisfies me forever? If we agree that [taste can be either good or bad](https://paulgraham.com/goodtaste.html), probably. It would mean that I've reached the ceiling of improvement on the design of this website, whatever that looks like.
